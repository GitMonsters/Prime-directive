ADVANCED REASONING CAPABILITIES:
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. CROSS-DOMAIN INFERENCE
   ──────────────────────
   Infer knowledge from one domain to another using analogies.

   Example:
     Query: "How does fluid behavior relate to plasma?"
     Reasoning: Magnetohydrodynamics treats plasma as conducting fluid
                → Same equation structures and conservation laws apply

   Supported Analogies:
     • Fluid dynamics ↔ Plasma physics (MHD analogy)
     • Quantum mechanics ↔ Quantum field theory (field extension)
     • Classical mechanics ↔ Electromagnetism (Lorentz force)
     • Thermodynamics ↔ Statistical mechanics (microscopic foundation)
     • Relativity ↔ Cosmology (spacetime evolution)
     • Particle physics ↔ Astrophysics (high-energy processes)

2. PREDICTIVE REASONING
   ────────────────────
   Predict physical outcomes given time scales.

   Example:
     Query: "What is the fate of the universe?"
     Time Scale: Long (years to cosmic)
     Prediction: "Dark energy dominates; exponential expansion"
     Uncertainty: "Final fate depends on dark energy equation of state"

   Time Scales:
     • Short (seconds/minutes): Immediate effects and interactions
     • Medium (hours/days): Evolution and response
     • Long (years/cosmic): Ultimate outcomes and steady states

3. CAUSAL REASONING
   ────────────────
   Trace causal chains in physical systems.

   Example:
     Query: "How does mass cause physical effects?"
     Domain: Relativity
     Effects:
       1. Curves spacetime
       2. Affects light paths
       3. Creates gravitational lensing

   Causal Chains for Each Domain:
     • Relativity: Mass → Spacetime curvature → Light deflection
     • Cosmology: Inflation → Flatness → Primordial structure
     • Particle Physics: Symmetry breaking → Mass generation → Yukawa couplings

4. UNCERTAINTY QUANTIFICATION
   ──────────────────────────
   Quantify measurement uncertainties and confidence levels.

   Example:
     Query: "How accurate is the Hubble constant measurement?"
     Measurement: hubble_constant
     Uncertainty: ±2.0%
     Confidence: 95%
     Sources:
       • Distance ladder calibration
       • Gravitational lensing effects
       • Supernova luminosity calibration

   Standard Measurements with Known Uncertainties:
     • Hubble constant (H₀): ±2% uncertainty, 95% confidence
     • Higgs mass: ±0.1% uncertainty, 99% confidence
     • Fine structure constant (α): ±1.5% uncertainty, 95% confidence

5. EXPLANATION GENERATION
   ──────────────────────
   Automatically generate physics explanations.

   Features:
     • Cites relevant laws with equations
     • Lists fundamental principles
     • Provides physical descriptions
     • Identifies related domains
     • Indicates complexity level
